Here if I say a product I mean a product/service.

I will continue repeating this until everyone and their mother realize how dependent they are on their boss for their survival because they didn’t continue to learn after (or before) leaving the school system.

If you don’t create a product to sell, you will continue selling a product for someone else.

The only reason you are making any form of an income right now is because you play one specific role in a company that makes the money by selling a product or service.

In other words, if you want to earn your freedom, first, you need an audience, and second, you need a product by which people voluntarily trade their money for the value placed in said product.

Long story short, build something you can sell or your survival, and those stress and emotions, will be like uncontrollable waves determined by a company.

This Is For You If...

  • You are tired of freelancing, coaching, or client work and want more control over your time and income. You want to work on your own projects, not someone else's.
  • You think monetizing your work with donations, sponsors, or YouTube ad revenue is a sustainable way to make a living (when it is unpredictable and can be ripped from you in a millisecond).
  • You have skills, interests, and expertise you want to monetize outside of a job that limits your income (and probably won't promote you).
  • You've tried starting a business but distract yourself with creating logos, building websites, and never get around to creating a good product (you know... the only thing that will make you money while you sleep).
  • You've launched a product before, but like most businesses, made close-to-zero sales because you didn't seek education or guidance to follow.
  • You think your interests aren't monetizable (and you don't want to seem salesy) so you prevent yourself from doing what you love.
  • You've finally realized that you have to sell something (for yourself, not for someone else) to have full control over your income.
  • You have no idea where to start, what to build, or how to stand out in a market flooded with courses, templates, and programs.

Who Is This Not For?...

  • Those that don't see the value in personal growth and positive impact.
  • Those that intentionally — or unintentionally — seek to harm others through negative content (certain memes, pornography, etc)
  • Those that have a distorted perception of money, sales, and taking the impact of what you put out into the world seriously.
  • On the business side of things — those that are set on pursuing a hyper-focused niche for freelancing, agency work, and other things.
